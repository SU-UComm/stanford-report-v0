Bioengineered tool cuts off cancer cell defenses

Sugar-coated proteins called mucins are implicated in many diseases, including cancer. A Stanford-led team has bioengineered enzyme-based scissors that selectively cut mucins off cancer cells, removing their “cloak of protection” from the body’s immune system.
